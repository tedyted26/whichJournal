About

This system is created by a Computer Engineering student of Universidad Europea de Madrid as the Final Degree Project (2023). Its main purpose is to aid new researchers in the search of potential journals or conferences in which they can share their work and knowledge in a specific field. This is done using natural lenguage processing and trained IA models to recommend the best options (from our own databases, updated regularly) for publishing and sharing content: the only information needed is a piece of the same work that is going to be published (Don't worry! We don't save it). We also take care of detecting untrustworty journals that only look for their own economic gain using information from Beall's list and journal rankings like SJR Scimago Journal & Country Rank.

Need clarifications?

Traditionally, journals were subscription based, meaning that the autor could publish for free but the contents would be locked behind a subscription fee for readers. Also, copyright of the article would be transfered to the publishing journal and every action regarding that article should seek permision from the journal.

Now, Open Access has gained popularity. Using this publishing model, autors pay an APC or Article Processing Charges for publication and readers have the contents for free. This fee depends on the journal and some other factors. Author's copyright in this case is preserved, using Creative Commons Licenses.

There are different open access types such as "Gold OA", "Green OA" and "Hybrid". In Gold OA articles are made available online in the journal's website with a CC license and paying an APC as described above. In Green OA, authors can upload their articles free of charge to online repositories or their own websites. Lastly, Hybrid models mix aspects from Subscription-based publishing and Open Access, making it possible for the authors to pay a publication fee only for specific articles while maintaining a Subscription-based model for the rest of them.

Bibliometric indicators are a group of metrics calculated using statistical methods with the purpose of analyzing publishing trends and information such as the citation impact of a given journal.

Impact factor is a popular metric calculated by Clarivate for journals in their Web of Science index that measures it's importance. It takes in consideration the number of citations of the journal's articles in the previous 2 years. The higher the result, the more important a journal is. Moreover, there is a variation of this impact factor called 5-year Impact factor, that calculates the number of citation in the last 5 years.

CiteScore is another bibliometric indicator similar to Impact Factor. This metric is calculated using data from Scopus on a yearly basis, and has more measured journals than Clarivate.

Journals that prioritize economic gain over quality, often missing necessary services or checks like peer review (review of the contents of a new article to publish by a group of proffesionals in the field). They also can try to get hold of the article's ownership. In WhichJournal, we mark potential predatory journals with a wolf icon. It's important to notice that this icon serves as a mere warning, to decide if the journal is actually predatory or not, is left to user's criteria and a few resources down below that help identify them:

Scientific conferences or workshops are events in which researchers of a given field gather to discuss academic work. They have benefits such as information exchange, feedback and visibility of one's research. They can be virtual, in-person or hybrid, and some requirements to participate are to submit an article of the given reseach area and pay the submission fees. Conditions may vary depending on the conference, the organization that holds it and the country it takes place.

Similar to Impact Factor, SJR Scimago Journal Rank is the average number of weighted citations in the 3 previous years of a determined journal. Quartils, on the other hand, are subdivisions of a category where the top 25% journals are Q1, top 25-50% are Q2, 50-75% Q3, and 75-100% are Q4. WhichJournal uses the SJR ranking and quartils from Scimago's database for all possible journals (some of them are not in the database). The best journals are the ones with higher SJR ranking and the ones that make up Q1 or Q2.

Other journal metrics shown in our recommendations are the acceptance rate, time to first decision, time to review, and time to publication:
  • Acceptance Rate: Percentage of manuscripts that get accepted for publication.
  • Time to first decision: Time that passes from the submission of the manuscript to the first notice the author receives about acceptance. If addmited, the manuscript goes for revision.
  • Time to review: Time that takes to review the accepted manuscript and decide to either accept or reject it.
  • Time to publication: Time that passes from being an accepted and reviewed manuscript to its first appearance as an online journal with a DOI code.
